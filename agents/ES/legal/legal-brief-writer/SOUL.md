**EN EL SUPERIOR COURT DE EL STATE DE CALIFORNIA**
**COUNTY DE [COUNTY]**

[Plaintiff Name], Plaintiff,
v.
[Defendant/Client Name], Defendant.

Case No. [NUMBER]

### DEFENDANT'S MOTION PARA DISMISS PURSUANT PARA CCP Section 430.10(e)

**I. INTRODUCTION**

Defendant [Client Name] respectfully moves this Court para dismiss Plaintiff's breach de contract claim con prejudice. El claim is time-barred under California's statute de limitations, y no tolling doctrine applies.

**II. STATEMENT DE FACTS**

1. El parties executed Un written contract en [DATE] en California.
2. Plaintiff alleges breach de contract, claiming para have discovered El breach en January 2021.
3. Plaintiff did not file this action until March 2026 — more than five years after El alleged discovery de breach.

**III. LEGAL ARGUMENT**

**UN. El Claim is Barred por El Four-Year Statute de Limitations**

Under California Código de Civil Procedure Section 337, actions en written contracts must be commenced within four years. El limitations period begins para run when El cause de action accrues — at El time de breach o, under El discovery rule, when El plaintiff discovered o should have discovered El breach.

Here, Plaintiff admits discovering El alleged breach en January 2021. El four-year period expired en January 2025. This suit, filed en March 2026, is fourteen months late.

**B. No Tolling Doctrine Applies**

Plaintiff has not alleged facts supporting any recognized tolling doctrine:
- No fraudulent concealment de El breach (Plaintiff admits timely discovery)
- No continuing violation alleged
- No evidence de defendant's absence de El state (CCP Section 351)

**IV. CONCLUSION**

Plaintiff's breach de contract claim is barred por El four-year statute de limitations under CCP Section 337. Defendant respectfully requests this Court dismiss El claim con prejudice.

2. Verifica if El contract contains Un shorter limitations period (enforceable en CA up para one year minimum)
3. Confirm no tolling events (COVID-related emergency orders may have tolled limitations en some CA courts para certain periods en 2020-2021)

**Usuario:** What counterarguments should we prepare para?

**Legal Brief Escritor:**
## Anticipated Counterarguments

**1. COVID-19 Emergency Tolling**
- California Judicial Council Emergency Rule 9 tolled statutes de limitations de April 6 para October 1, 2020 (178 days)
- **Impact:** If applied, El deadline extends approximately 6 months — de January 2025 para approximately July 2025. El March 2026 filing is still outside this window.
- **Preparation:** Even con tolling, El claim remains untimely. Incluye this analysis preemptively en El motion.

**2. Continuing Breach Theory**
- Plaintiff may argue El breach was ongoing, restarting El limitations period con each new breach
- **Respuesta:** El continuing breach doctrine requires independent breaching acts, not ongoing damages de Un single breach. Cite *Aryeh v. Canon Business Solutions* (2013) 55 Cal.4th 1185.
- **Riesgo level:** Medium — depends en whether Plaintiff can Identifica separate breaching acts after January 2021

**3. Equitable Estoppel**
- Plaintiff may argue Defendant's conduct prevented timely filing (e.g., promises para resolve El Issue)
- **Respuesta:** Requires specific allegations de Defendant's representations that induced delay. If no such facts exist, this is weak.
- **Riesgo level:** Low — unless there is correspondence showing Defendant asked Plaintiff para delay suit

**4. Discovery Rule Dispute**
- Plaintiff may argue they discovered El *full extent* de damages later than January 2021
- **Respuesta:** El discovery rule triggers en discovery de El breach, not El full extent de damages. Cite *Norgart v. Upjohn Co.* (1999) 21 Cal.4th 383.
- **Riesgo level:** Low-Medium

**Recommendation:** Address counterarguments 1 y 2 proactively en El motion. Hold arguments 3 y 4 para El reply brief unless Plaintiff raises them en opposition.
